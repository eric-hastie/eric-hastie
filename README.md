# Hi - I'm Eric 👋

**Enterprise sales professional who builds his own tools.**

I'm an Account Executive, not an engineer. I've been using AI pair-programming tools to build working software around the problems I actually have - finding the right opportunities, researching accounts from primary sources, and turning messy real-world data into something a team can use. The AI does the typing; the ICP, the process design, and the QA are on me.

Everything here ships with the same discipline I'd bring to a territory: scope the problem, verify the data, and tell the truth about what works (and what didn't).

## The work

**[remote-ae-job-hunt](https://github.com/eric-hastie/remote-ae-job-hunt)** - I treated my job search like a sales pipeline: defined an ICP, built an automated top-of-funnel, and hand-verified every record before it entered the pipe. The pivot that shaped it: scraping kept burying the good fits, so I stopped scraping and started verifying.

**[territory-radar](https://github.com/eric-hastie/territory-radar)** - The same engine, pointed at the other side of the desk: an account-planning board that scores a territory on live buying signals (hiring, funding, leadership moves) and tracks weekly momentum. [Live demo](https://eric-hastie.github.io/territory-radar/)

**[portfolio-trading-knowledge-library](https://github.com/eric-hastie/portfolio-trading-knowledge-library)** - The leader of a trading community asked me to turn 4 years of Discord history (40k screenshots, 161k messages) into a queryable knowledge library for beginners. The repo publishes the tooling and the reasoning - none of the community's data. Knowing what never leaves the CRM is part of the job.

**[yt-research-toolkit](https://github.com/eric-hastie/yt-research-toolkit)** - Founders and executives say what they actually care about on podcasts and conference stages - it just never gets written down. One command turns that into a clean, citable transcript library. This is how I prep for discovery calls (and how I'd prospect with intelligence nobody else in the deal has).

**[onyx-detail-collective](https://github.com/eric-hastie/onyx-detail-collective)** - A full go-to-market motion for a small business: market segmentation, 79 researched prospects ranked into a priority hit list, outreach playbook, pricing, and a 90-day plan to recurring revenue. [Live site](https://eric-hastie.github.io/onyx-detail-collective/)

**[hotshot-market-entry](https://github.com/eric-hastie/hotshot-market-entry)** - Market-entry research for a small logistics venture: 50 operator interviews analyzed at scale, load-board rate benchmarking, and unit economics honest enough to kill my own favorite scenario. Discovery and qualification, applied somewhere with wheels.

**[personal-assistant](https://github.com/eric-hastie/personal-assistant)** - A local AI assistant that answers questions about my own life (the unsaved address from a 2019 text, everywhere I've traveled from photo GPS). A new capability is literally one Python file. All personal data stays on my machine - the repo ships none of it.

**[photo-viewer](https://github.com/eric-hastie/photo-viewer)** - I noticed I was wasting time opening and closing screenshots one at a time, so I built a faster way the same day. Small tool, real time saved.
